Topic: On-campus housing lottery — how it actually works (student-compiled FAQ)

Q: Is the housing lottery actually random?
A: Partially. Berkeley Housing assigns a random lottery number to every applicant within their class year ( freshman, sophomore, etc.). You pick room groups before the lottery opens. When your number comes up, you select from remaining rooms in real time. Priority groups exist: re-applicants who lived on campus the prior year get priority over new applicants in the same class year. Disabled students and athletes have separate assignment processes outside the general lottery. So the number is random, but priority tiers mean it's not a flat random draw across all students.

Q: When does the lottery run?
A: Typically late April for returning students, late May-June for incoming freshmen. Exact dates are posted on the Housing portal — check housing.berkeley.edu in March. Missing the application deadline means waitlist only.

Q: Can you game the lottery?
A: Students report that forming a group of 4-6 compatible roommates before lottery day helps because you can claim a full suite together when your best number in the group is called. Solo applicants get whatever single beds remain, often in triples converted from doubles.

Q: What if you get a bad number?
A: Waitlist movement happens throughout summer as people cancel. Join the Facebook group "Berkeley Housing Swap" — students trade assignments. Off-campus housing search should start in parallel by March if your number historically wouldn't reach your preferred unit (Unit 1, Unit 2, Foothill, Clark Kerr).

Q: Unit preferences from students:
Unit 1 and Unit 2: closest to campus, loudest, most social. Foothill: quieter, uphill, nicer rooms. Clark Kerr: far but peaceful, smaller community. Blackwell Hall: newest, premium pricing, still on meal plan.

Q: Meal plan requirement:
Most on-campus dorms require the default meal plan (15 or 19 meals per week depending on building). Apartments like Martinez and Clark Kerr apartments have reduced meal plan options.
