Source: r/berkeley — "Off-campus housing southside honest reviews"
Thread collected February 2025

u_rent_watcher: Southside (Telegraph area) is the default off-campus zone. Pros: walking distance to campus, restaurants, BART at Downtown Berkeley. Cons: noise, older buildings, pest issues in some units. Always tour before signing.

u_mold_warning: WARNING — several units in the Dwight-telegraph corridor have had mold complaints posted on the Berkeley Tenant's Union site. Ask landlords about ventilation and recent remediation. If you smell musty during tour, leave. Document everything with photos at move-in.

u_studio_hunter: Expect $1400-1800/month for a studio, $900-1200 per person for a shared 2BR as of 2025. Prices drop slightly if you sign 12-month lease starting August vs January sublet.

u_landlord_review: Gaia Building — mixed reviews. Good location, some units have plumbing issues. Red Building on Durant — loud but cheap. Haste Street apartments — verify which company manages; same address different quality.

u_roommate_tips: Use Cal Rentals portal for verified listings. Avoid wiring deposits before seeing lease. Scams spike every July. The International House sublet board is legit for short-term.

u_utilities: Most southside leases don't include utilities. Budget $80-120/month for electric/gas/internet split among roommates. Laundry in basement coin ops — count quarters.

u_parking: Street parking permits from City of Berkeley required. Spots are scarce after 6pm. Don't bring a car unless you have a assigned spot in lease.

u_inspection: City of Berkeley has rental housing safety program — you can report unhabitable conditions. Know your rights before move-in.
